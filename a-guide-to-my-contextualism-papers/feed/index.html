You undervalue the “Explanation and defense” paper. Sure — it’s accessible and provides some history and context and that’s all to the good. Plus, it gets the argument started for those coming to the issue fresh. I think it’s good not only for undergraduates but for grad students starting out and for professionals who want to get started on questions like “what’s up with all this contextualism stuff anyway?”. Of course deeper discussions are found elsewhere (everyone will realize that and your guide here will serve as a pointer for some to the newer and deeper discussions). But that now “getting old” paper is a great starting point both because of the accessibility and because it partly presents contextualism as an overall view and not merely as an approach to skepticism (which, shockingly, doesn’t concern all philosophers the way it does us!).
