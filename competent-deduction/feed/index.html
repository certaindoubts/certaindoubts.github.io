But let me ask you about the first conjunct: i.e., that a felicitous mistake is surely a defeater. Here’s an example with a felicitous mistake (or at least a felicitous event), yet it doesn’t seem to be a defeater. Suppose I begin an inference and (unknown to me) I have some neurological disruption that typically diminishes the capacity to reason properly. But in this particular case–by pure chance– the disruption actually increases my reasoning ability. I complete the deduction and reach the right conclusion. Suppose further that I would have reached that conclusion anyway, had the disruption not happened. There’s a felicitous event in the inferential process that by chance leads me to the right conclusion. But I nonetheless don’t seem to reach the right conclusion by chance. I would have gotten there anyway had the event not happened. Do you count that sort of felicitous mistake or event as a defeater?
