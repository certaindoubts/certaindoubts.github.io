]]> By: Ralph Wedgwood http://certaindoubts.com/three-kinds-of-justification/#comment-8192 Tue, 09 Dec 2008 13:55:08 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=886#comment-8192 Thanks, John! But actually, no, I really meant what I said. The second conjunct of the antecedent of the principle is really meant to be the proposition that p does in fact logically entail q.

Assume for the sake of argument that the degrees to which you are propositionally (or “ex ante”) justified in believing the relevant propositions can be modelled by a probability function. Now, if p logically entails q, the probability of q must be at least as high as the probability of p. So the degree to which you are justified in believing q must also be at least as high as the degree to which you are justified in believing p. That is, SINGLE-PREMISE CLOSURE is true.
