]]> By: Gilbert Harman http://certaindoubts.com/harman-and-sherman-on-belief-and-intention/#comment-1723 Tue, 03 May 2005 18:46:33 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=318#comment-1723 Jon, to answer your two questions. First, (T2) is a
“variant” of a causal theory only in the sense that it
appeals to explanation rather than causation. (Harman,
Thought, p. 130.)

Second, part of the idea behind the Self-referential
Guarantee in Belief Principle (SRGBP) is that when you
explicitly believe that P, you are implicitly committed to
more than the content of P, where what you are committed to can
be brought out by considering when it is and when it is not
rational to have that explicit belief. (Change in View,
chapter 5.)

The implicit content of an intention seems to be
self-referential (discussed in Harman, “Practical Reasoning,”
reprinted in Reasoning, Meaning, and Mind) and
I am inclined to think that the implicit content of full
belief is also self-referential.

–Gil
