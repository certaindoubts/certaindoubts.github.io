]]> By: Adam http://certaindoubts.com/donald-kagan-on-postmodernism-history-and-philosophy/#comment-1787 Wed, 18 May 2005 16:35:17 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=327#comment-1787 So is philosophy the “queen of the humanities” by virtue of the fact that philosophers can claim that nearly any thought or argument that has universal application is philosophical? Surely, saying that philosophy (qua the accumulation of knowledge by means of reason) is the queen of the humanities is a truism. Indeed, philosophy in that sense is queen of the entire academic enterprise–after all, the highest academic degree is the “doctor of philosophy” degree. But do philosophers (i.e. the people who study and teach in departments of philosophy) generally believe that philosophy (qua the discipline that investigates the range of topics discussed in said departments) has pre-eminence over the humanities or the academy in general? Now that’s a claim we could have an argument about!
