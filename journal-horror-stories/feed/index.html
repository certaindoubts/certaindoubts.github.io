]]> By: Anonymous Epistemologist http://certaindoubts.com/journal-horror-stories/#comment-10517 Fri, 15 Jan 2010 04:21:06 +0000 http://el-prod.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=1245#comment-10517 I submitted a piece to an unnamed Springer journal in mid-October and it has not been assigned a referee. I asked a friend about this and he said that he’s had a paper at this same journal since March and it hasn’t been assigned a referee either.

I don’t think this is a Springer problem, per se. I’ve had things submitted more recently to two other Springer journals that were assigned to referees in under a week.

This isn’t just a problem with the status updates. I emailed to ask if a referee had been assigned a month ago and was told that the editor would be contacted so that it would be assigned a referee. No status change.

Someone could say that this journal is just swamped, but I don’t think that this particular journal receives so many more submissions from other journals that it should take this long to get refs. PPR is swamped, but they get refs assigned to papers in under a week. Phil Quarterly took about a month to review this same paper and I received thorough and helpful comments from a referee.

What gives? Are others having similar problems with any Springer journals? Is this even a problem, or is 3+ months a reasonable time frame to get from editor to referee?

Jeremy, at least accountability has permitted you to present yourself as the sort of person who will own up to an accidental and honest mistake. Maybe it’s identified you as someone who makes them- but that just makes you human as they say. Accountability smells great!

Back to blasting JPhil though. I must confess I’ve not yet had anything bad from them but I am extremely worried by the idea that they post arrogant/rude comments in reply. More so than I am over their editing practices even since I think this kind of stuff could spread.

JPhil is the kind of journal with a reputation that is such that it would be a good thing for your career (as an early career sort of person) if you publish in. You might reasonably submit your first article there. Getting stuff like that in reply might make younger scholars that this is the norm or industry standard- it comes from a well regarded (sort of) place and if they’ve no previous experience of publishing.

Maybe I’m being naive about the idea that this might ever come about, but for me this is a massive concern over comments.
