]]> By: Jeremy Fantl http://certaindoubts.com/a-case-against-some-forms-of-anti-intellectualism/#comment-10358 Fri, 04 Dec 2009 03:27:44 +0000 http://el-prod.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=1621#comment-10358 Hi Jon.

You say,

“the claim that you believe p isn’t a premise you can use in practical inference”.

It’s not a premise you can use in theoretical inference, either, is it? Because that too “would require considering or entertaining p, and you are rationally constrained not to do that”. So, if this is supposed to end up showing that you can know that you believe p without being rational to act as if you believe p, then it will also show that you can know that you believe p without being rational to believe as if you believe p. For instance, that you believe p cannot be used as a reason to believe that either you believe p or the moon is made of green cheese.

(For what it’s worth, I would think the variety of knowledge-action link worth defending would have it that if you know that p, then if p isn’t usable as a premise in practical reasoning, it’s not due to your epistemic weakness with respect to p. And that knowledge-action link doesn’t seem threatened by your example.)
