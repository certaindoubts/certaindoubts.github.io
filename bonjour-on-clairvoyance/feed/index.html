]]> By: thomas basboell http://certaindoubts.com/bonjour-on-clairvoyance/#comment-2367 Mon, 08 Aug 2005 02:20:43 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=401#comment-2367 I think we should be equally dismissive of the *existence* of ETI and ESP. This is because there is no evidence for either. That is, we should not believe there are such things. I think we should be equally supportive of research into the existence of these phenomena.

There is an important sense in which, given no evidence of the existence of the object, a research project into it is necessarily a sort of experimental investigation of its conditions of possibility.

I disagree, though uneasily, with (4) because I think natural science can only say that life on other planets could occur to the extent that conditions on that planeta obtain which are *like those on earth*. This is roughly the same position that ESP research is in, ESP is like ordinary sensory perception except different.

I have met very few people who know enough about astronomy and biology on the on hand and psychology and neurology on the other to be able to say that natural science is in a better position to explain how ETI could work than ESP.

The objects “intelligence” and “perception” strike me as essentially wedded to the terrestrial, sensory context. Until a competing phenomenon presents itself, I any talk of how it “could work” strikes me as wholly speculative.

Again, however, I’ve settled on provisionally “allowing” such speculative research (in whatever sense it is any of my business), subject to the ordinary material constraints on how many research programmes there can be.
