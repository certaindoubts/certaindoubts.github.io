I think you said in the paper:
a) If the rest of your belief system renders p improbable, then p is “incoherent with” your belief system.
b) “Every epistemology with a fighting chance” agrees that whenever p is incoherent with the rest of your beliefs, it’s irrational to believe p.

So here’s a scenario. I’m rolling three normal dice. I roll the dice, and seem to see them come up triple 6. I immediately (non-inferentially) believe that I am seeing three 6’s face up on the dice. But the probability of this happening, on the *rest* of my beliefs, is only .0046. So I restore coherence to my belief system by deciding that I am not actually seeing that. Rational?
