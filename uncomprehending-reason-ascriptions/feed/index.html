It’s hard for me to imagine someone actually saying RA1 while intending it in the de dicto sense, though. The bit at the end about Nawal having no idea what a filibuster is seems to mark the ascription as de re.

But suppose it is de re. There’s still something odd about it. If she has _no idea_ what a filibuster is, (i.e., it’s not just that she doesn’t know what “filibuster” means but that she has no notion that there could be something which matches the description of what you and I know as a “filibuster”) then why should Josh identify the object of her worry as a “filibuster?”

There are other ways to hold up a bill, after all. But let’s set that aside and pretend a filibuster is the only way to hold up a bill. And let’s even allow that Nawal might say “I’m worried the Republican might do something to hold up the bill.” In this case, she’s got a worry the description of which uniquely identifies filibusters. Even in this case, if she has no idea what a filibuster is, then wherever she got the notion that someone could hold up a bill, she didn’t get it from the fact that there are filibusters. (Her worry may somehow have actual filibusters in its causal history, but not in any way that could make her worry actually about filibusters–for that would mean she had “some idea” what a filibuster is, however vague or deferential.)

So her worry doesn’t seem to be connected to filibusters at all–it just happens, by coincidence, to match a description of filibusters. And so to say that she’s worried about a filibuster seems odd (if not inconsistent).

It seems odd in the same way the following seems odd. Someone who has never heard of an elephant reports to me “I had a strange dream about a large, grey four footed animal with big ears and a long tentacle-like appendage for a nose.” And I reply “Oh, you were dreaming about an elephant!” Well, no he wasn’t–he has no concept of elephants. It’s just that his dream had content which, when explicated descriptively, _happens_ coincidentally to match the content of “elephant”.

In the case as I’ve elaborated on it, the intuition that the ascription is odd probably relies on a causal-historical account of meaning. I’m not sure if that’s what’s behind the intuition in your original case, though I suspect it might be.

]]> By: Luis Rosa http://certaindoubts.com/uncomprehending-reason-ascriptions/#comment-9999 Tue, 29 Sep 2009 01:30:49 +0000 http://el-prod.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=1448#comment-9999 Hi,
The concept of a filibuster doesn’t appear in the Nawal’s talk or reasoning. It appears only in the reason ascription, in Josh discourse. When Josh uses this concept, he knows what he’s referring to, and he knows that Nawal can’t, in the actual state of things, relate this concept with its referent.
But that doesn’t mean that Nawal can’t refer to what Josh refers using the concept of a filibuster. He can refer to the same thing by means of other concepts, wich will make his reasons to believe.
And so, the appearance of inconsistency is caused by this difference in the semantic domain of Josh (who is making the ascription) and Nawal (who is ascribed with reasons).
