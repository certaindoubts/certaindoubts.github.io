]]> By: Chris Tucker http://certaindoubts.com/new-entry-at-sep-logical-pluralism/#comment-39450 Mon, 22 Apr 2013 18:29:18 +0000 http://certaindoubts.com/?p=3976#comment-39450 I probably think of epistemology’s scope differently than most. I think of it as a kind of value theory–the study of epistemic value, whatever that is. Consequently, I don’t think belief and truth are included under epistemology proper, as many seem to think. Nor do I think logic should be included under epistemology because it is too formal and doesn’t intrinsically involve normativity, at least not anything obviously like epistemic normativity. Yes, epistemologists rely on formal frameworks to develop their views, but, with the exception of probability theory, it’s not clear to me that they rely on logic to any greater degree than does, say, metaphysics.
