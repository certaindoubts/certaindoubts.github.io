I don’t want to rule out alternative arguments for disjunctivism having to do with, say, content. But, the normative sort of argument I don’t think can do the work.

“So I’m pretty sure that things “blankly external to you” can affect your epistemic status.”

Yes, I think that’s right. Actually, once we have on hand the totality of conditions by virtue of which something right is right and then sort out the conditions that determine what normative reasons there are, what enablers there are, etc…, it’s harder to see the attraction of the view that makes all the normatively significant conditions internal.
