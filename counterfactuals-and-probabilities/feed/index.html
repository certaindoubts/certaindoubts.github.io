On the standard L/S semantics, this is just what you get: if A and B are true, then A []-> B and B[]-> A. And on a standard probability theory, things can happen that have no chance of happening (just let the space of alternatives be infinite…). ]]> By: Mike http://certaindoubts.com/counterfactuals-and-probabilities/#comment-1935 Tue, 07 Jun 2005 00:41:51 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=346#comment-1935 *The extreme view at the top end is that a subjunctive is not true unless the probability of consequent given antecedent is 1*

Does this say that ‘A []-> B’ is true only if Pr(B/A)= 1? I’m not sure what else you might mean here. But it is hard to see why anyone would think it is true. Suppose a transparent God is one who in general lets you know why he does things. If there were a transparent God then I would know the reason for most evils. So ‘A []->B’ true. But conditional probability is not counterfactual. Rather it is read, given that there *is* a transparent God, we see the reason for most evils. But of course Pr(B) is quite low and that conditional probability is not high. So Pr(B/A) is not high. There are any number of other counterexamples.

*The extreme view at the bottom end is that a subjunctive is true when antecedent and consequent are both true, no matter what the probability*

No matter what the probability of what? It can’t be that the antecedent A and consequent B of ‘A []-> B’ are both true and yet the conditional probabilty Pr(B/A) is zero. Pr(B/A) is nothing but Pr(B & A)/Pr(A).
