]]> By: jon kvanvig http://certaindoubts.com/evidence-and-propositions-youve-never-considered/#comment-350 Mon, 19 Jul 2004 22:19:55 +0000 http://fleetwood.baylor.edu/certain_doubts/?p=58#comment-350 Matt W.: here’s the defense of claim you italicize. Doxastic justification is propositional justification plus proper basing. So if you can be d.j. in believing ~p while p.p. in believing p, it would have to be possible for you to be propositionally justified in believing p and propositionally justified in believing ~p.

I think of propositional justification in terms of what fits with your total evidence, both the evidence that is the content of your beliefs and the evidence that is part of your experience. But i don’t think you need this particular conception to reject the idea that propositional justification can obtain for p and for ~p at the same time.

There is one qualification here if one thinks of belief as a triadic relation. Then it will be possible to have justification for p under one mode of presentation and justification for ~p where p is presented under a different mode of presentation. The counterexamples to PP can be restricted, however, to cases where the mode of presentation is the same.
