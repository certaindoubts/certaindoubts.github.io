Williamson in K&L maintains that if we grant that an assertion is defective when false, we have to admit that it would be improper to assert a claim that the speaker isn’t reasonable in taking to be true and for that reason we have to think that the C that warrants assertion is not T. But having granted that the correct judgment that it is improper to assert what one doesn’t have good reason to think is true, it doesn’t seem Williamson’s attempted derivation automatically goes through.

Suppose it did, however. Surely the defender of the K account realizes that the same problem haunts their view. It is possible to reasonably think one doesn’t know a claim one does in fact know. Assertion under such conditions seems improper but according to defenders of the K account, we cannot for that reason revise our initial claim about what warrants assertion.
