I learn just enough computer technology to do what I need and leave the rest to those who find machines, and not (just) arguments, intrinsically interesting. Here’s a trick that works quite well (especially here at UAH where high-tech saavy is common, even among liberal arts students). Of course, when they fail, you sometimes have a bit of explaining to do when the official guys come around! But that doesn’t happen very often. My best advice came from a former major who now works as the computer factotum at a local firm specializing in modeling complex hydraulic processes. Since he’s still a good friend, he bails me out when my home machine malfunctions.
